Executive Summary & Theoretical Context

SQLBarber is a query workload replication framework designed to synthesize SQL query templates that statistically approximate the cost distribution of an observed, ground-truth database workload. Its core pipeline operates in three stages: (1) profiling, where an instrumented DBMS captures the query optimizer's estimated cost for every executed query and bins those costs into discrete cost ranges; (2) template extraction, where representative structural patterns — parameterized SQL skeletons — are identified for each cost bin; and (3) synthetic workload generation, where the framework instantiates concrete queries from those templates with the goal of reproducing the original cost-bin histogram. The fidelity of this reproduction determines whether the synthetic workload can stand as a valid proxy for the original in DBMS performance studies, regression testing, and hardware provisioning experiments.

Matching the cost-bin distribution of original query workloads is not merely a statistical nicety — it is the foundation of synthetic benchmark validity. Database management systems exhibit highly non-linear performance characteristics: operator pipeline selection, buffer pool pressure, index selectivity, and join order sensitivity all vary discontinuously across cost tiers. If a synthetic workload over-concentrates queries in a mid-to-high cost range while failing to represent low-cost fast-path queries in proportion, the resulting benchmark will systematically overestimate CPU and I/O pressure. This phenomenon — distribution drift — invalidates comparative benchmarking and can lead to incorrect conclusions about query optimizer tuning and memory allocation.

This report analyzes a 33-template replication run against a 10-bin cost spectrum (0–10k optimizer cost units). The original workload exhibits a bi-modal character, with peaks at 3k–4k (27 observations) and 5k–6k (29 observations). The synthetic output reveals significant distributional drift: over-concentration of 13 templates in the 5k–6k bin, the complete elimination of 2k–3k coverage, and three templates (T3, T10, T29) yielding costs entirely outside the defined measurement window — pointing to systematic calibration gaps in the template instantiation engine's parameter sampling strategy.

Deep-Dive Analytical Observations
Original workload characteristics
  • Bi-modal distribution with dominant peaks at 3k–4k (27 obs.) and 5k–6k (29 obs.), representing 61.5% of total workload volume.
  • Empty gaps at both 1k–2k and 4k–5k — the optimizer rarely produces plans in those cost ranges for this schema and data distribution.
  • Low-cost presence: 20 observations in 0–1k reflect fast, index-scan or trivial aggregation queries that any valid synthetic benchmark must preserve.
  • High-cost tail: 21 observations in the 8k–10k range indicate heavy analytic queries with multi-join or large sort operations.
Synthetic workload characteristics
  • Massive over-concentration in the 5k–6k bin with 13 templates (39.4%). This single bin absorbs queries originally from 0–1k, 2–3k, 3–4k, 8–9k, and 9–10k.
  • Unexpected 4k–5k coverage: 2 templates appear in a bin with zero original observations, creating synthetic noise with no real-workload counterpart.
  • Complete absence of synthetic templates in 2k–3k — a bin that had 9 original observations entirely lost in the synthetic run.
  • High-cost collapse: Only 3 templates in 8k–10k versus 21 original observations — a reduction of 85.7% for heavy analytical query coverage.
⚠ Critical anomalies — requires immediate attention
T3 — Out of Bounds
Originally spanning 2k–3k / 3k–4k. Synthesized instances produced optimizer cost estimates entirely outside the 0–10k window. Likely a predicate explosion or unbounded Cartesian join introduced during parameter substitution.
T10 — Out of Bounds
Originally in 5k–6k / 6k–7k. Out-of-bounds cost generation suggests the template contains a subquery or windowed aggregate whose cardinality estimate explodes when realistic parameter values are substituted.
T29 — Out of Bounds
Originally at 9k–10k — already at the ceiling. Any upward cost perturbation during parameter sampling pushes it beyond the 10k boundary. High sensitivity to value selectivity; cap parameters near minimum selectivity.
T12 — Extreme Upward Shift (+5k to +7k)
Original bins: 2k–3k / 3k–4k. Synthetic landing: 7k–8k, 8k–9k, AND 9k–10k simultaneously — spanning three of the highest bins. Likely a join with dramatically underestimated cardinality. Decompose into narrower sub-templates, each targeting a specific cost tier, and cap selectivity parameters.
Bidirectional instability noted. Shifts are not systematic in one direction: T6, T9, T17, T18, T25, T30 shift downward (high-cost originals → 0–1k), while T11, T33, T15, T21–T23, T27 shift upward (low/mid-cost → 5–6k). This rules out a single directional calibration bias — parameter sampling variance is the root cause.
📋 Replication fidelity verdict & calibration recommendations
Overall verdict: Low fidelity. With only 3 perfect matches (9.1%) and 81.8% of templates bin-shifted, this synthetic run does not constitute a valid workload proxy for the original. Do not use this output as a benchmark substitute without remediation.
Rec 01
Constrain parameter sampling
Replace uniform random sampling with histogram-aware sampling drawn from column statistics. Ensure synthetic values preserve the selectivity profile observed during profiling. Use ANALYZE output to bound value ranges.
Rec 02
Add cost feedback loop
After each template instantiation, use EXPLAIN to verify optimizer cost falls within the target bin. If cost falls outside, resample and retry (max N attempts). This rejection-sampling approach will eliminate OOB cases.
Rec 03
Stratify templates by bin
Enforce per-bin template quotas matching the original distribution's proportional shares. Cap 5k–6k assignments and force allocation to 2k–3k, which currently has zero synthetic coverage despite 9 original observations.
Rec 04
Decompose unstable templates
T3, T10, T12, T29 exhibit extreme cost variance. Split each into narrower structural variants with tighter parameter constraints. T12 spans 5+ bins and should be split into 3+ sub-templates each targeting one cost tier.
Rec 05
Validate on held-out slice
Partition the original workload 80/20. Extract templates from 80%, validate the synthetic distribution against the 20% hold-out. Use Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) as the primary fidelity score. Target EMD < 0.15.
Rec 06
Preserve structural gaps
The absence of observations at 1k–2k and 4k–5k is semantically meaningful. Enforce hard exclusion zones in the synthetic generator to prevent population of empty bins, especially the 4k–5k region.
